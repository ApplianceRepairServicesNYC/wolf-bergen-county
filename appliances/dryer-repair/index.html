Heating Element Burnout

Wolf electric dryer heating element repair addresses the most common cause of no-heat complaints. The heating element consists of coiled wire that glows red-hot when electricity passes through it. Over time, these coils can break from thermal stress or develop shorts to the housing. When the element fails, your dryer tumbles but produces no heat. Our technicians test element continuity, inspect for visible breaks, and install genuine Wolf replacement elements rated for your specific model throughout Hackensack, Paramus, and Bergen County.

Thermal Fuse Failure

Wolf electric dryer thermal fuse repair resolves a common safety device failure that stops dryer operation completely. The thermal fuse is a one-time safety device that blows when the dryer overheats—once blown, it cannot be reset. Common causes include clogged vents restricting airflow, failed thermostats, and cycling thermostat issues. Our technicians replace blown thermal fuses AND diagnose why overheating occurred to prevent repeat failures. Thermal fuse service throughout Teaneck, Fort Lee, and Bergen County.

High-Limit Thermostat Trips

Wolf electric dryer high-limit thermostat repair addresses the safety device that cuts power when temperatures exceed safe levels. Unlike the thermal fuse, the high-limit thermostat can reset—but repeated trips indicate underlying problems. Restricted airflow from clogged vents, failed cycling thermostats, or heating element issues cause overheating. Our technicians test thermostat function, identify the overheating cause, and repair all contributing factors throughout Ridgewood, Englewood, and Bergen County homes.

Timer Motor Failures

Wolf electric dryer timer repair fixes the mechanical or electronic timers that sequence dry cycles. Timer problems cause cycles to stop mid-dry, run continuously without advancing, or skip portions of the program. Mechanical timers have internal motors and contact points that wear over time. Electronic timers on control boards can have component failures. Our technicians diagnose timer issues and replace failed components, restoring proper cycle sequencing throughout Glen Rock, Bergenfield, and Bergen County.

Belt Breakage

Wolf electric dryer belt replacement restores drum rotation when the drive belt breaks. The drum belt wraps around the drum and connects to the motor via an idler pulley. Belts wear, crack, and eventually break from normal use. When the belt breaks, the motor runs but the drum doesn't turn. Belt replacement is a common repair our technicians complete quickly, typically including idler pulley inspection since worn pulleys accelerate belt wear. Belt service throughout Dumont, New Milford, and Bergen County.

Drum Roller Wear

Wolf electric dryer drum roller repair eliminates the thumping, squeaking, or rumbling sounds caused by worn drum support rollers. Most dryers use two to four rollers supporting the drum's rear and sometimes front. When roller bearings wear or flat spots develop, you'll hear rhythmic noise during tumbling. Our technicians replace drum rollers as a set, ensuring balanced support and quiet operation. Drum roller service throughout Mahwah, Ramsey, and Bergen County electric dryer installations.

Door Switch Problems

Wolf electric dryer door switch repair fixes the safety mechanism that prevents operation when the door is open. The door switch signals the control board that the door is closed—if it fails, the dryer won't start even with the door shut. Switch contacts wear from repeated door opening and closing. Our technicians test switch continuity and replace failed switches, restoring safe operation throughout Tenafly, Cresskill, and Bergen County homes.

Control Board Issues

Wolf electric dryer control board repair addresses electronic failures affecting dryer operation. The main control board manages all functions—accepting user inputs, controlling heating, monitoring sensors, and timing cycles. Board failures cause erratic behavior, error codes, cycles not completing, or complete failure to operate. Power surges and component failures are common causes. Our technicians diagnose board problems accurately, replacing boards when necessary throughout Wyckoff, Franklin Lakes, and Bergen County.

Motor Failures

Wolf electric dryer motor repair fixes the drive motor that rotates the drum and powers the blower wheel. Motor failures cause the dryer not to run at all, run intermittently, or make humming noises without turning. Motors can fail from bearing wear, winding shorts, or start switch problems. Our technicians diagnose motor issues, determining whether the motor itself has failed or if other components like the belt or idler are binding the motor. Motor service throughout Bergen County.

Gas Valve Solenoid Failures

Wolf gas dryer valve solenoid repair addresses one of the most common gas dryer failures. Gas valve solenoids are electromagnetic coils that open the gas valve when the igniter gets hot enough. Most Wolf gas dryers have two solenoids—one for the main valve and one for the secondary. These solenoids can fail intermittently, working when cold but failing when hot. Our technicians diagnose solenoid failures using amp draw testing and replace failed coils throughout Hackensack, Paramus, and Bergen County.

Igniter Problems

Wolf gas dryer igniter repair fixes the component that lights the gas burner. The igniter glows orange-hot, reaching temperatures high enough to ignite gas. When igniters weaken, they glow but don't get hot enough to open the gas valve or ignite the gas. Completely failed igniters don't glow at all. Our technicians test igniter amp draw—weak igniters draw less current even while glowing. Igniter replacement restores reliable heating throughout Teaneck, Fort Lee, and Bergen County.

Flame Sensor Malfunctions

Wolf gas dryer flame sensor repair addresses the safety device that monitors burner operation. The flame sensor detects heat from the burner flame—if it doesn't sense heat, it shuts off gas flow as a safety measure. Failed sensors can incorrectly shut off gas even when flames are present, causing intermittent heating. Sensors also fail by not detecting flame absence, though this is less common. Our technicians test and replace flame sensors throughout Ridgewood, Englewood, and Bergen County.

Burner Assembly Clogs

Wolf gas dryer burner cleaning removes lint and debris that accumulate in the burner assembly. Unlike lint in the exhaust system, burner area lint affects combustion quality and can be a fire hazard. Clogged burner ports cause uneven flames, yellow flames (instead of blue), and incomplete combustion. Our technicians clean burner assemblies, inspect gas orifices, and ensure proper combustion throughout Glen Rock, Bergenfield, and Bergen County gas dryer installations.
